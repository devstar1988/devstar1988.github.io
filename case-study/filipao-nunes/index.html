An overview of the studio's portfolio and details about the studio's brand and business needs that shaped the site's design

Business Details

In the past couple of years the studio has penetrated the high-end residential and business architecture markets and has achieved growing success. The studio is starting to compete against well-known national firms on large projects and was in need of an online platform that exposed their new brand and body of work.

The studio worked closely with Nation Visuals to design the studio's branding. The studio’s logo is adaptable and can be applied in three different orientations to reflect the studio’s multi-disciplinary focus. The brand uses a sans serif type, a dark gray / white color palette, and a lot of negative space.

Portfolio Details

The portfolio is designed with a focus on promoting the studio's new brand and creating an immersive experience into the studio's work. A visual styleguide was designed based on the studio's brand to serve as a reusable library of visual properties to build the site's UI components.

The landing page was designed to provide a bold introduction to the studio’s brand, what they do, and their work. The landing page starts off with the studio's logo to help users connect the logo with the studio's name.

After the studio's logo and caption, the last section features an interactive gallery that displays three select projects. Each project represents one of the studio’s three focus areas—architecture, interior design, sculpture—to promote the studio’s multi-disciplinary expertise.

The Projects section lists the studio's portfolio and orders projects by category and provides a category filter menu for faster project search.

Project detail pages provide an immersive experience into the project by displaying a list of images that walk the user through the studio's work. Each project detail has an introductory section composed of a hero image and project description to give user’s context about the project’s purpose and mission.

The core of the project’s detail page is in its content which is composed of 5 reusable components. Each component was designed to display different types of content. Some components work great for vertical shots, other components work better for full-width landscape shots. The studio has the flexibility to design each project detail to ensure each detail section displays the project in the best possible format.

The Studio section provides an overview into the studio’s founder, the services it offers, and the agencies it collaborates with. The studio’s about page ends with a brief section introducing the studio’s offices and is followed by a job opportunity section that links to the site’s contact page to remind users about available internship and job opportunities.

The Contact section displays the studio’s basic contact information and displays a list of available job and internship opportunities. Each job links to a detail page that provides job and application details.

Next Steps

The studio is working with Nation Visuals on gathering project images and site content and will touch base mid January 2017 to start designing the project detail pages and start the site’s development.

Insights

Through this project I faced a series of obstacles that made the project a little bit more challenging than I expected. These challenges served as opportunities to grow and learn about the importance of research, the value of prototypes, and the power of functional CSS.

Exploring without Direction

For the first round of designs I explored with several layouts and structures and created 3 responsive prototypes so my client could interact with the designs. The prototypes were very helpful, as they help my client communicate more clearly and helped define the portfolio's structural and visual direction.

Defining the Site’s Content

When I started doing some design explorations, I didn’t know who the studio was or what they wanted to communicate. I reached out to my client and asked if I could work with his team for a couple of days. Working from the studio gave me enough context to get a feel for the studio’s personality and business needs and enabled me to design a portfolio the studio was happy with.

Fast, Reusable Styling

I needed to build 3 prototypes as quickly as possible. I had been reading about the benefits of functional styling so I decided to give Tachyons a try. By building with Tachyons I learned that function-based styling eliminates specificity problems and is a great solution for building scalable, reusable, and predictable CSS libraries.
